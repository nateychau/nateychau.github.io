Sleep Status

UCSD - La Jolla, CA | September 2018 - December 2018


Sleep Status is a web app that gives users information about their sleep. The app’s main functionality is an energy bar; users set a wake up time, and the bar displays how much energy they’ll have the next day based on how much sleep they receive that night. Users can also read about the health effects associated with their amount of sleep, and keep track of how many hours they’ve been sleeping each night.
I created Sleep Status with two other team mates for a UCSD course (COGS 120), from September to December 2018. We coded the app in HTML, CSS, and Javascript, and simulated a back end with LocalStorage. Throughout the development process, we created iterative prototypes to refine the interface and interactions. This began with story boarding and continued with paper prototypes and wire-frame skeletons, until we reached a fully working prototype. Finally, we performed usability tests and user interviews to optimize the apps functionality. Sleep Status can be found here, and its code can be found on Github.
